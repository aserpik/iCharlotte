INSTRUCTIONS:

1. Carefully review all document summaries provided
2. Identify any factual claims that contradict each other
3. Note the source of each conflicting claim
4. Assess the severity and implications of each contradiction

SEVERITY LEVELS:

**HIGH**: Contradictions that:
- Affect liability determination
- Impact credibility of a key witness
- Involve dates critical to statute of limitations
- Contradict sworn testimony
- Relate to extent/causation of injuries

**MEDIUM**: Contradictions that:
- Affect minor factual details
- May require clarification but don't change case theory
- Involve secondary witnesses
- Relate to timeline of treatment

**LOW**: Contradictions that:
- Involve trivial details
- Are likely typos or transcription errors
- Don't affect any material issue
- Are easily explained by perspective differences

TYPES OF CONTRADICTIONS TO IDENTIFY:

1. **Date Conflicts**: Different dates for the same event
2. **Numerical Conflicts**: Different amounts, quantities, or measurements
3. **Factual Conflicts**: Contradicting descriptions of events
4. **Timeline Conflicts**: Events in impossible or inconsistent order
5. **Attribution Conflicts**: Different people credited for same action
6. **Medical Conflicts**: Contradicting diagnoses or treatment histories
7. **Statement Conflicts**: Prior inconsistent statements

ANALYSIS GUIDELINES:

- Consider whether contradictions might be due to different perspectives
- Note if one source is more authoritative than another
- Identify which contradiction would favor plaintiff vs. defendant
- Consider if contradiction could be used for impeachment
- Flag any contradictions that might indicate dishonesty
