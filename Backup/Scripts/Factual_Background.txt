Mandatory Style, Tone, and Formatting Requirements (Based on Examples):
	1. Heading: Do NOT include the heading "FACTUAL BACKGROUND". Start directly with the text.
	2. Opening Phrase: Start the paragraph immediately with "This matter arises out of..." or "This matter arises from...". Choose the one that flows best, but stick to these specific phrasings.

	4. Content Order & Structure: Generally follow this sequence:
		○ Identify the type of incident (e.g., "a trip and fall," "a motor vehicle collision," "an incident," "allegedly negligent repairs," "bodily injuries suffered by... as a result of an incident").
		○ State the date and, if available and relevant, the approximate time of the incident. Use precise dates or date ranges as provided in the source.
		○ Specify the location of the incident, including address/intersection and city/state. Often designate the location with a parenthetical label like ("Incident Location"), ("the Property"), or ("Subject Intersection"). Use the most appropriate label based on context.
		○ Introduce the key parties involved:
			§ Clearly identify the Plaintiff(s), using full names and designating them with ("Plaintiff") or ("Plaintiffs"). Use parenthetical abbreviations for names if appropriate (e.g., "Robert Petersen ('Plaintiff')"). If there's a decedent, identify them similarly, e.g., ("Decedent").
			§ Identify the key Defendant(s), often naming them and sometimes grouping them (collectively, "Defendants") or identifying their role (e.g., driver, employer, property owner, contractor).
			§ Mention relevant relationships if stated (e.g., employer-employee, landlord-tenant, contractor-subcontractor).
		○ Briefly describe the core allegation or the mechanism of the incident (e.g., "alleges that he tripped and fell... due to insufficient lighting," "negligently failed to yield and struck a vehicle," "struck by a cart... being pushed... by Defendants' employees," "lost control on a wet, soapy ramp," "fell on the ramp outside the restaurant entrance," "repairs... were improperly completed," "collided with a vehicle," "made a left turn in front of him, causing a collision").

		○ Briefly mention the types of damages sought if specified in the source (e.g., "past and future medical expenses, loss of earnings," "general and special damages," "wrongful death damages," "loss of consortium").
	5. Formatting Specifics:
		○ Use parentheses () extensively for abbreviations (like 'Plaintiff', 'Decedent', 'Defendants') and location labels (("Incident Location"), etc.) exactly as shown in the examples.
		○ Capitalize proper nouns (names of people, companies, specific locations, cities, states).
		○ Use quotation marks "" only for specific terms or phrases allegedly used or directly quoted from the source (e.g., "improper wheel stop"), or sometimes for intersection names if that's the established convention in the source.
		○ Ensure correct grammar and punctuation, maintaining a professional standard.
	6. Conciseness: The paragraph should be dense with information but remain a concise summary. Avoid unnecessary words or elaboration beyond the core introductory facts. Aim for a length and density similar to the provided examples.
	7. Source Adherence: Base the paragraph exclusively on the information provided by the user in the prompt or uploaded documents.
