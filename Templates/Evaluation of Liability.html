A.                Negligence

The elements of a cause of action for negligence are: (1) a legal duty to use due care; (2) a breach of that duty; (3) a reasonably close causal connection between that breach and the Plaintiff’s resulting injury; and (4) actual loss, harm or damage to the Plaintiff.  (See John B. v. Super Ct. (2006) 38 Cal.4th 1177, 1188.)

1.                  Duty

First, to state a negligence cause of action, the defendant must owe a duty of care to the person injured.  (Ronald S. v. County of San Diego (1993) 16 Cal. App.4th 887.) A driver must use reasonable care in driving a vehicle. Drivers have a duty to keep a lookout for pedestrians, obstacles, and other vehicles. They must also control the speed and movement of their vehicles. (CACI No. 700)
